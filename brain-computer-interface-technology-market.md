<p><p>The Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) technology market is primarily divided into invasive and non-invasive types. Invasive BCIs involve surgical implantation of devices directly into the brain, offering high accuracy and direct neural interaction, typically used in clinical applications for severe neurological disorders. Non-invasive BCIs, on the other hand, utilize external sensors to interpret brain signals without surgery, making them safer and more accessible for consumer applications like gaming and wellness monitoring. Both segments are rapidly evolving, catering to diverse needs.</p></p>
