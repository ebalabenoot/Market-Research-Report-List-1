<p><strong>The Low-Dosage Hydrate Inhibitors (LDHIs) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Anti-Agglomerate (AA) Inhibitors</li><li>Kinetic Hydrate Inhibitors (KHI)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Low-Dosage Hydrate Inhibitors (LDHIs) are specialized chemicals used in oil and gas production to prevent hydrate formation, a common problem in hydrocarbon transportation. The market comprises two main types: Anti-Agglomerate (AA) inhibitors, which prevent hydrate particles from clumping together, and Kinetic Hydrate Inhibitors (KHI), which slow down the hydrate formation process. These inhibitors are essential for maintaining flow assurance, reducing production downtime, and minimizing operational costs, thus playing a crucial role in enhancing overall efficiency in harsh environments.</p></p>
