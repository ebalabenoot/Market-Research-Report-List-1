The enzyme coupling method involves the use of enzymatic reactions to amplify the signal of creatine kinase, providing high sensitivity and specificity. Colorimetric assays involve the measurement of absorbance changes in a sample, enabling quantification of enzyme activity visually. Both methods are widely used in clinical laboratories for diagnosing and monitoring muscle and heart disorders.</p></p>
