<p><ul><li>Viral Gastrointestinal Infections</li><li>Bacterial Gastrointestinal Infections</li><li>Parasitic Gastrointestinal Infections</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The gastrointestinal infection testing market is categorized into three main types: viral, bacterial, and parasitic gastrointestinal infections. Viral infections, such as norovirus and rotavirus, are primarily diagnosed through polymerase chain reaction (PCR) and antigen tests. Bacterial infections, including Salmonella and E. coli, are identified via culture and serological testing. Parasitic infections, like Giardia and Cryptosporidium, are diagnosed through stool examination and microscopy. Each category requires specific testing methodologies to ensure accurate diagnosis and effective treatment.</p></p>
